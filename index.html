ISWC 2018 workshop

Tuesday, October 9th, 2018

Monterey, California, USA

Many of the Social Good topics can benefit from the sort of unplanned, cross-domain, rapid-prototyping analysis that Linked Data makes possible. We’ve learned that measuring topics like poverty, health, economic opportunity and equality can be substantially more nuanced by considering broader categories of data than organizations traditionally collect for their own use. Past conferences have had individual presentations on topics that involve Semantic Web for Social Good. This workshop is intended to present multiple topics, to better identify intersections, overlaps, opportunities for accelerated advancement, and opportunities for shared abstractions.

Program

The format of the workshop will be a carefully curated mixed event, with an intentional selection of speakers based on papers that are deemed most likely to lead to meaningful break-out discussions and identification of next steps.

Opening

Short, diverse panel discussion of the shared needs of Social Good data analytics. The goal of the moderator will be to identify the common threads about Ontologies, Reasoning, and Recognizing Impact that will be tracked throughout the Workshop.

Presentations

A series of presentations, from submissions, on Semantic Web Social Good projects (research, government/NGO, and/or corporate) across domains such as health, economics, and equality.

Break-Out Session

Splitting the participants into groups to identify intersections, overlaps, opportunities for accelerated advancement, and opportunities for shared abstractions across the projects presented, and in support of the broader goals described at the Opening Panel.

Closing Discussion

Very brief review of the day to provide a background for the Summary of the Break Out Session results and Consideration of Next Steps (e.g., a write-up synthesizing themes from the Workshop; possible establishment of a standards interest group; new collaborations).
